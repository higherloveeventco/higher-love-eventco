St. Johns · Portland, Oregon

Get married in St. Johns.

A genuinely beautiful neighborhood with small-town character, river views, and the prettiest bridge in Portland. Most of the city is already a busy, expensive place to get married. St. Johns is more affordable, and far less discovered.

Photo: Lindsay Blair Photography
Why St. Johns

A neighborhood built for gathering.

St. Johns runs on the kind of closeness most neighborhoods lost a long time ago. The florist knows the baker. The taphouse sends people down the street to the wine bar. Business owners show up for each other, cheer each other on, and pitch in when someone needs a hand. It still feels like a small town, where people genuinely want to see one another do well. When you get married here, that whole web of relationships is quietly working in your favor.

I plan weddings across Portland and the greater Pacific Northwest, but St. Johns is where I am based and where I have the deepest relationships. When couples choose to marry here, I can lean on a whole neighborhood of people I know and trust to make the day feel local, cohesive, and genuinely theirs.

A bit of history

More than a hundred years ago, St. Johns was its own independent town at the confluence of the Willamette and Columbia rivers, named after James John, an early resident who donated land to build a public school and was known for his generosity. It eventually merged with Portland, but it never lost the small-town feeling. That is still what you feel walking the main street today.

Why St. Johns over other Portland neighborhoods

A neighborhood, not a venue.

St. Johns is not a wedding venue. It is a neighborhood with several places to get married within it. Weddinglandia for an intimate chapel ceremony. The St. Johns Inn, a beautiful historic property where you bring in everything yourself and build the day from scratch. And Cathedral Park, which sits under the gothic arches of the St. Johns Bridge and is one of the most stunning outdoor ceremony locations in Portland.

It is also one of the more affordable areas in Portland for weddings. Most of the reception options here are neighborhood restaurants and bars that do full buyouts, not traditional event venues with rental fees stacked on top of food and beverage minimums.

Cathedral Park itself is free to enter and always open to the public. Getting married there requires a permit from Portland Parks and Recreation, which runs a few hundred dollars. The setting looks like it costs considerably more than that.

St. Johns wedding venues

Where to get married in St. Johns, Portland.

From a historic inn to neighborhood breweries, taphouses, and restaurants, St. Johns has a venue for every kind of celebration. Some host the whole day, ceremony and all, while others are made for the reception and the party that follows.

St. Johns Inn

A 1910 Colonial Revival home on a bluff above the Willamette, shaded by century-old Heritage oaks, with a sprawling lawn and river views a short walk from Cathedral Park and the bridge. It is a blank canvas: you build the day from the ground up, with no required packages or vendor restrictions, and your people can stay the night in the Inn's guest rooms. Higher Love is a preferred planner here.

Cathedral Park Deck & Staircase

The iconic spot beneath the St. Johns Bridge, where the gothic arches frame the river and the light turns golden in the evening. One of the most photographed ceremony locations in Portland. Permitted weddings are unavailable while bridge repair work is underway, with a tentative reopening for 2027. Read the full Cathedral Park guide.

Elope under the St. Johns Bridge

You can elope at Cathedral Park with no permit required, as long as it is just the two of you, your officiant, and two witnesses. The deck and staircase beneath the bridge are where most couples say their vows, and it is hard to imagine a more stunning place to do it.

Getting here

Logistics for out-of-town guests.

St. Johns is in North Portland, about 6 miles from downtown. It is most easily reached by car. Here is what your guests need to know.

Getting here

St. Johns is most easily reached by car. From downtown Portland, take US-30 W (St. Helens Road) north along the river. Rideshare is widely available. TriMet bus lines 16 and 75 serve the neighborhood from downtown.

Where to stay

St. Johns Inn has guest rooms on site, ideal for the couple and close family. Most guests will stay in downtown Portland or NW Portland, both 15 to 20 minutes away. The Holiday Inn Columbia Riverfront in North Portland is the closest full-service hotel to the neighborhood.

Getting around

Everything in St. Johns is walkable once you are here. The main street, Cathedral Park, Weddinglandia, and most of the venues on this page are within a few blocks of each other. For guests coming from hotels, a rideshare or shuttle is the easiest option. Northwest Navigator runs guest shuttles and I can coordinate that as part of your planning.

Planning a spring wedding? The St. Johns Bizarre happens every second Saturday in May: a neighborhood-wide festival with a parade, live music, over 100 vendors, and a beer garden taking up six city blocks. Worth knowing before you set your date.
