St. Johns · Portland, Oregon

Get married in St. Johns.

St. Johns has the charm, the romance, and the realness, without the price tag of a wedding destination. A riverfront park, a main street of locally owned shops and pubs, and the bridge that gave Cathedral Park its name. The lesser-known side of Portland, ready to welcome you.

Photo: Lindsay Blair Photography
Why St. Johns

A neighborhood that already feels like a celebration.

St. Johns sits at the northern tip of Portland, wrapped around a bend in the Willamette and anchored by the cathedral spires of the St. Johns Bridge. It has the rare thing a lot of wedding destinations are missing: a real sense of place. A walkable main street, independent bars and restaurants, a historic inn, and a riverfront park that looks like it was built for photographs.

I plan weddings across Portland and the greater Pacific Northwest, but St. Johns is where I am based and where I have the deepest relationships. When couples choose to marry here, I can lean on a whole neighborhood of people I know and trust to make the day feel local, cohesive, and genuinely theirs.

Photo: Lindsay Blair Photography
The venues

Where to get married in St. Johns.

From a historic inn to neighborhood breweries, taphouses, and restaurants, St. Johns has a venue for every kind of celebration. Some host the whole day, ceremony and all, while others are made for the reception and the party that follows.

Ceremony + Reception
01

St. Johns Inn

A historic inn on the main street, and one of my favorite spaces in the neighborhood. Intimate, full of character, and walkable to everything St. Johns has to offer. The one indoor spot on this list that hosts the whole day, ceremony and reception both.

Photo: Lindsay Blair Photography
02

Cathedral Park Deck & Staircase

The iconic spot beneath the St. Johns Bridge, where the Gothic arches frame the river and the light turns golden in the evening. One of the most beautiful ceremony and reception settings in Portland.

Currently unavailable · tentative 2027 reopening

Elope under the St. Johns Bridge

You can elope at Cathedral Park with no permit required, as long as it is just the two of you, your officiant, and two witnesses. The deck and staircase beneath the bridge are where most couples say their vows, and it is hard to imagine a more stunning place to do it.

Ceremony Only
03

Weddinglandia

A beloved St. Johns wedding chapel run by Jasmine and Zach Nye, and my partner in the neighborhood. A beautiful, ready-to-go ceremony space, with the celebration to follow planned by me.

04

Cathedral Park Amphitheater

An open-air amphitheater within Cathedral Park, available now for ceremonies. A lovely option for couples who want the drama of the park and the bridge for their vows.

Reception Only
05

Mosaic Taphouse

A relaxed taphouse for couples who want their celebration to feel like the best night out with everyone they love. Food on-site by Chém Gió.

06

Stormbreaker Brewing

A neighborhood brewery with room to gather, raise a glass, and celebrate. Great beer, good food, and an easy party atmosphere.

07

Leisure Public House

A welcoming public house with the kind of easy, convivial spirit that makes a celebration feel effortless.

08

Bar Nouveau

A beautiful restaurant offering full buyouts for intimate weddings, with elevated food and a lovely space. Up to around 40 guests.

09

Urban German

A second-floor restaurant right by the St. Johns Bridge, with killer views, beautiful natural light, and a large deck patio strung with cafe lights. Also available for catering.

10

Sticky Wicket

A neighborhood spot that does full buyouts, just as good for the reception as it is for the rehearsal dinner or the after party.
