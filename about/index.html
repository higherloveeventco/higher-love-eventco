Portland born & raised · St. Johns local

I’m Kylie, founder of Higher Love Event Co. A native Oregonian, mom, wife, and passionate event specialist. I’ve spent more than half a decade working in the event industry, and I can’t imagine doing anything else.

My experience in this industry has taken me through roles in venue sales, catering sales, luxury wedding coordination, and large-scale event production including corporate events and galas, multi-day themed expos, cultural celebrations, and casino and hotel holiday installations. Sub-coordinating for other planners across the PNW deepened my understanding of how different planning styles, systems, and workflows operate, and sharpened my own in the process. Working the vendor side of the table taught me how venues and caterers think, what matters to them, and how to communicate in a way that actually moves planning forward. The result is a well-rounded, experienced event professional with a deep understanding of the Portland wedding industry.

I’m highly detailed, deeply organized, with an eye for design. I’ve channeled all of that into a planning formula built on years of firsthand knowledge of the Portland wedding scene, one that streamlines the process, narrows the options, and makes every decision easier to make. Because hiring a planner should reduce the decisions you’re making, not just help you manage them.

I’m based in Portland, but I’ve had the privilege of coordinating weddings everywhere from the jungles of Tulum to the Utah desert. My favorite weddings are the ones I plan here in the beautiful Pacific Northwest. I’ve coordinated at nearly 20 different venues across wine country, the Portland metro, and the Columbia River Gorge.

I’m proud to call the St. Johns neighborhood home, and I believe it’s one of Portland’s best kept secrets for beautiful, soulful weddings. The neighborhood has everything: Cathedral Park, the Willamette River, the iconic St. Johns Bridge, and a robust vendor scene made up of incredibly talented and passionate people. With the Bridge’s upcoming reopening and the forthcoming opportunity to reserve the Cathedral Park deck and staircase (slated for 2027), St. Johns is primed to become the place to get married in the Portland metro, and I can’t wait to play a role in making that happen.

Wherever your wedding takes place, my goal is always the same. A planning process without overwhelm. A wedding day you get to enjoy. Free of worry, free of loose ends, and nothing pulling you out of the moment. That doesn’t happen by accident. It happens because the right work was done long before the day arrived.

What I believe

The values behind every event

01 — Transparency

No hidden fees. No fine print.

Pricing is published before you reach out. The process is clear from day one. You always know exactly what you’re getting, what it costs, and what happens next.

02 — Simplicity

Less to decide. More to enjoy.

Wedding planning doesn’t have to mean hundreds of tabs and an inbox full of vendor quotes. The formula exists to narrow the field, surface the best options, and make every choice feel manageable.

03 — Expertise

You’re in experienced hands.

Half a decade in the industry. 20 venues across the Pacific Northwest and experience in every role from vendor, to production, to coordinator. That breadth of experience is what makes the difference between a planner who reacts and one who anticipates.

04 — Presence

A wedding day you get to enjoy.

Not a day spent fielding questions or watching the clock. A day where you get to eat, laugh, cry, dance, and take it all in — and where your family gets to celebrate right alongside you, instead of coordinating every detail of the day. A third party (that’s me!) has the details handled from start to finish.
