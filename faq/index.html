The Model

Traditional planning models — day-of, partial, full-service — are built for couples who want to be closely involved in every decision. There's nothing wrong with that. But until now, there hasn't been a model designed specifically for the couple who is overwhelmed, short on time, or simply doesn't want wedding planning to be their part-time job.

Higher Love fills that gap. Budget & Fit

The honest answer is: it depends on a lot of factors. The numbers below reflect a full, traditional wedding — a dedicated venue, outside catering and bar, photographer, DJ/MC, hair and makeup, and a dance party reception. They do not apply to full restaurant buyouts, elopements at Cathedral Park, or other non-traditional formats where the cost structure looks very different. For those, reach out and we'll talk through what makes sense for your situation.

Venue: Starting at $4,500–$7,000 for Portland Metro; $7,000+ for Hood River/Gorge venues. Popular or in-demand venues can run $10,000–$15,000 or more.

Catering: $75 per guest is the minimum for a licensed, insured, and properly staffed caterer. For a seated dinner with full service, budget $100–$150 per guest. For 75 guests, that's $7,500–$11,250 for food alone before bar.

Bar: There are two types of bartending service and it's important to know the difference before you start getting quotes. Regardless of the bar style you choose, Oregon law requires a licensed OLCC certified bartender at any event where alcohol is served.

Option 1: You buy the alcohol, they serve it
You purchase all the alcohol yourself and the bartending company provides the staff, equipment, and service. A good bartending company will send you a detailed shopping list based on your guest count and drink preferences — some will even shop with you. This is often the more budget-friendly option and gives you full control over what's being served. Budget $1,000+ for the bartending service itself, then separately budget $1,500–$4,000+ for alcohol depending on guest count and drink selection.

Option 2: They bring everything
The bartending company supplies both the staff and the alcohol. In this case there will be a bar minimum — a dollar amount you are on the hook for regardless of how much your guests actually drink. If your guests consume less than the minimum, you still pay the minimum. If they drink more, you pay more. Bar minimums are also standard at most venues that include bar service — if you're getting married at a venue with an in-house bar, expect a minimum.

Tableside wine or champagne service during dinner is almost always an upgrade with an additional cost, typically requiring dedicated attendants. If this is important to you, ask about it specifically when getting quotes.

Photography: Starting at $2,400–$2,500 for 6–8 hours from a licensed, insured Portland-area photographer with documented experience and full gallery delivery. Mid-range photographers run $3,500–$5,000. In-demand and editorial photographers start at $6,000–$6,500+ for 8 hours. Most packages do not include retouching — budget an additional $100/hour if requested. The photographer you fall in love with on Instagram will almost certainly be at the higher end of this range.

DJ/MC: $1,500 minimum for ceremony, MC, and reception coverage — though some companies start at $1,250 for a 4-hour package. Additional hours are typically billed at $200–$250/hour. A full evening reception of 5–6 hours runs $1,500–$2,000 for a mid-range DJ. Experienced, in-demand DJs run $2,500–$3,500. Party lighting is almost always a separate add-on — uplights, moving heads, lasers, and dance floor lighting packages typically run $350–$900+ depending on the setup. If you want your venue to feel like a real dance party, budget for lighting separately.

Hair & Makeup: $200–$400 per person is the starting range for a licensed, experienced artist — but in-demand and highly sought-after hair and makeup artists in the Portland market run $500–$700+ per person. Trials (a separate appointment at full price), travel fees, and early morning fees add to the total. A real-world example: a party of five at a recent Higher Love wedding came to $2,925 — before the trial. For a couple plus a wedding party of four, budget $2,500–$5,000+ depending on the artist. If you have your heart set on a specific aesthetic or a particular artist whose work you've seen on Instagram, book early and budget accordingly — the best ones fill up a year or more in advance.

Florals: Starting at $2,500 for a simple, minimal look. A full floral installation with ceremony arch, centerpieces, and personal flowers runs $5,000–$10,000+.

Officiant: $300–$600 for a licensed, experienced officiant who will meet with you in advance, help craft your ceremony, and file your marriage license. Many couples choose to have a friend or family member officiate instead — Oregon makes this easy, as anyone can become ordained online through organizations like Universal Life Church at no cost. Either way works beautifully. What matters is that whoever is standing up there knows you, means it, and has a valid Oregon officiant registration on file before the ceremony.

Videography: $2,500–$6,000+ for a professional wedding videographer with full-day coverage and edited highlight film delivery. Videography is one of the most commonly regretted skips — photos capture the look of your wedding, but video captures the sound, the movement, and the feeling. In-demand videographers in Portland book just as far in advance as photographers.

Transportation: $500–$2,500+ depending on distance, vehicle type, and number of runs. If your ceremony and reception are at different locations, or if you're hosting out-of-town guests, budget for shuttle service. Guest transportation is both a hospitality gesture and a safety measure when alcohol is being served.

Cake & Desserts: $500–$1,500+ for a custom wedding cake from a Portland bakery. Price varies by tier count, design complexity, and flavor. If you're doing a dessert table or alternative to cake, budget similarly.

Stationery: Printed save the dates, invitations, envelopes, and day-of signage can run $300–$1,500+ depending on paper stock, printing method, and design complexity. But digital is worth considering seriously — e-save the dates and e-invitations are more sustainable than paper goods, reduce paper waste, and eliminate the carbon footprint of mailing hundreds of envelopes across the country. They also cost significantly less. Your Higher Love planning package includes digital save the date and invitation templates designed to match your tablescape collection, plus matching day-of signage templates. All templates are edited in Canva and can be ordered and printed directly through Canva — no third-party print shop required. If you love the look of paper goods, budget $500–$1,500 for a print run. If you go digital, your stationery cost drops to near zero.

Attire & Alterations: Easy to underestimate. Dress alterations alone run $300–$800. Budget separately for attire, accessories, and alterations for both partners.

Gratuity & incidentals: Budget at least $1,200 for tips, unexpected costs, and day-of incidentals. Tipping vendors is standard practice and is not included in most vendor contracts.

A realistic starting budget for a Portland wedding with 50–75 guests is $20,000 — and that's a lean, well-planned event with mid-range vendors and minimal florals. The average Portland wedding runs $28,000–$35,000. If you're seeing magazine-worthy tablescapes, lush florals, a live band, and a stunning venue, that's a $40,000–$60,000+ wedding. For Hood River/Gorge weddings, add 15–20% to all of the above. $20,000 is a floor, not a ceiling.

Higher Love works exclusively with licensed, insured, and experienced vendors — because your wedding day deserves nothing less. The minimums above reflect the real cost of that standard in the Portland and Hood River markets.

Think of your wedding like a symphony. Your coordinator is the conductor — but the musicians still have to show up and know what they're doing for the show to go on. No amount of coordination can compensate for a vendor who is underqualified, understaffed, or unreliable.

If the numbers above feel out of reach right now, that's okay — it just means the timing might not be right yet, and I'd rather you know that now than after you've booked. If you're close but have questions, reach out. I'm happy to talk it through.
