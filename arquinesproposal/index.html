We'll connect at most once a month by email, phone, or video — but only when there's something meaningful to review. Long-lead planning has natural stretches of waiting, and I won't manufacture meetings for the sake of it. Once we hit the six-week mark before your wedding, we shift to weekly check-ins in whatever format works best for you.
When I need information from you — menu preferences, bar offerings, DJ song choices, or any decision I can't make on your behalf — I'll batch it and send everything in one message rather than trickling separate emails your way. Every request will come with enough context to make the decision feel straightforward, not overwhelming.
7
Final stretch.
Once RSVPs are in, I build your seating chart, finalize your floor plan, and confirm final counts with all vendors. From there we move into the final meetings: a timeline session to walk through the full day minute by minute, a ceremony planning session, and a final venue walkthrough. We wrap up with a final details meeting to confirm everything is locked — then I coordinate and run your rehearsal.
8
Show up and enjoy your wedding.
I'm on-site with two coordinators from setup through the last song — and we stay until teardown is complete and the last vendor has left.

About venue + rentals

Venue and rental costs are closely linked. Some venues include tables, chairs, dishware, and other basics — others require sourcing everything separately. A venue with less infrastructure will typically have a lower base cost, but you'll spend more on rentals to fill the gaps. A venue that includes the basics will cost more upfront, but you may not need to rent much at all. Once your venue is confirmed, we'll reassess the rental budget together.
