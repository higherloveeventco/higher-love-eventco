Option 1  ·  My Top Pick

Jupiter NEXT

Hotel + wedding venue on E. Burnside — urban, modern, and entirely your own for the night.
Why I picked this one for you

Jupiter NEXT is the only venue where all five of your preferred dates are available. With the NEXT Level package, the entire 2nd floor is yours for the night — Secret Garden for your ceremony, Flex Lounge for cocktail hour, and the Arium Ballroom for dinner and dancing. No transitions between buildings, no room flips, no one wheeling chairs past your guests.

But the reason this is my top pick goes beyond the wedding day itself. Jupiter gives you and your guests the most relaxed, seamless wedding weekend of any venue on this list. Your guests fly in, check into the hotel, and they're done. No hunting for accommodations near the venue, no coordinating rides, no worrying about how they're getting home at the end of the night. With 148 rooms across both properties, everyone is taken care of the moment they arrive.

When guests aren't stressed about getting there and getting home, the whole night feels different. They explore Portland during the day, they show up relaxed, they stay late. And when the last song ends, everyone just walks upstairs.

Option 2

Castaway Portland

A 1929 warehouse near the Willamette — industrial, warm, and endlessly photogenic.
Why it's #2

Castaway is stunning and the drape reveal is one of the most theatrical moments of any venue on this list. But there are a few meaningful reasons Jupiter edges it out for you specifically.

First, there's no hotel on site. For a wedding with a lot of guests flying in from California and Hawaii, that matters — it adds coordination, transportation, and end-of-night logistics that Jupiter eliminates entirely.

Second, catering flexibility. Castaway works from a preferred vendor list rather than being fully open, which gives us less room to bring in the Filipino cultural food elements that are important to you.

The flow is also more involved than it looks on paper. During cocktail hour, the ceremony chairs have to be moved out to the sidewalk, around the side of the building, and into the draped-off reception area. Some guests will linger in the courtyard after the ceremony — they always do — and they'll see the flip in progress. At Jupiter, that transition never exists. Guests move between spaces on the same floor and nothing is ever reset around them.

Jupiter is the stronger choice for your guest list. But Castaway is a truly beautiful space, and if you find yourself drawn to it, it's absolutely worth seeing in person.

Option 3

The Aerie at Eagle Landing

A stone-clad event venue overlooking the Eagle Landing Golf Course — lush, romantic, and tucked away in Happy Valley.
Why it's #3

The Aerie is genuinely lovely — outdoor garden ceremony with two pergolas and big open views, a chandelier-lit Marquee Tent for cocktail hour, and a Grand Ballroom for dinner and dancing. Everything flows naturally on the property. The in-house catering and bar means fewer vendors to coordinate, and almost everything is already included: tables, chairs, linens, glassware, tableware, a dance floor, and staffing. It makes the logistics simpler.

It's #3 primarily because of location. The Aerie is in Happy Valley — noticeably further southeast than Jupiter and Castaway. For a wedding with a lot of guests flying in from California and Hawaii, that's meaningful. It's a beautiful drive, but it adds distance and logistical complexity for out-of-town guests that the other two venues don't have.

The catering is exclusive and in-house. The kitchen has some flexibility — I'd ask specifically whether they can work in any Filipino specialty items. But it's not the same as Jupiter's fully open policy, where you can bring in any caterer you want. One nice thing: tiramisu is already on their dessert menu at $6/guest.

If the garden setting calls to you, it's worth seeing. In person, I think you'd feel it immediately one way or the other.

What this means for your $50k ceiling

The shared costs table shows the full range for every line item outside the venue itself. At the low end of every range, all three venues come in under $50,000 — Jupiter and Castaway around $45k, Aerie around $43k with shuttle. These are floors built on careful vendor selection across every category.

At the high end, all three venues land in the $65,000–$70,000 range regardless of guest count — the difference between 150 and 175 guests is about $3,000–$4,000 at the high end, and about the same at the low end. Catering, florals, and photography are the three categories with the most room to move in either direction. Every decision there is a dial we can turn together.

These are low estimates built on careful vendor selection. Locking in your guest count early is the single most important thing you can do for your budget — it affects almost every other number on this page.

A note on these numbers: All estimates use the low end of each vendor range. Photography, DJ, florals, and catering all have room to flex up or down. The goal is to show you the floor — what's achievable if we're strategic.

The biggest budget lever is open catering. Jupiter and Castaway both allow you to bring in any caterer, which means we can shop the market and find the best value for your guest count and menu. The $75/head catering figure in the tables above is the floor — achievable with the right caterer and strategic menu selection. Most Portland caterers run $95–$120/head for a full wedding dinner with staffing. At Aerie, catering is in-house and the price is fixed.

On bar: Beer and wine is the most effective way to control bar costs at any venue. The moment you add cocktails and spirits, the bar bill climbs significantly regardless of which venue you choose. Sticking to beer and wine keeps bar costs predictable.

On florals: Florals are one of the most scalable line items in any wedding budget. The $5,500 figure in these estimates covers a ceremony arch, modest centerpieces, and personal flowers — it's achievable with the right florist and seasonal blooms. Full floral buildouts with large installations, premium flowers, or dense centerpieces can easily reach $8,000–$12,000+. Portland florists typically have a $3,000–$5,000 minimum, so the $5,500 figure assumes mid-range design scope.

On F&B minimums: Every caterer has a minimum spend — that's standard across the industry. The goal is simply to choose a caterer whose minimum aligns with your guest count and budget, which we'll work through together.

Terms worth knowing.

Wedding planning has its own language. Here's a quick reference for the terms that come up most in this document and in conversations with venues.

Site Fee

The flat rental charge for using the venue space. This is separate from catering, bar, and other vendor costs — think of it as the price of the room itself.

F&B Minimum

Food and beverage minimum — the least amount you must spend on catering and bar combined. Standard practice across the industry. Most guest counts at 150+ will meet or exceed it naturally.

Open Catering

A venue policy that lets you bring in any licensed caterer you choose, rather than working from a required or preferred list. Open catering gives you full flexibility on cuisine and pricing.

Preferred Vendor List

A list of vendors the venue has vetted and recommends. You're not always required to use them, but some venues require you to choose from the list for catering and bar.

Exclusive Caterer

When a venue has one required catering partner and outside caterers are not permitted. The menu, pricing, and service style are set by that company — there's no ability to shop elsewhere.

Service Charge

A percentage added on top of catering and bar costs, typically 20–25%. This is separate from a gratuity and goes to the venue or catering company, not individual staff. Always factor this in when reviewing catering quotes.

BYO / Corkage Fee

BYO (bring your own) means you supply the alcohol rather than purchasing through the venue. Some venues charge a corkage fee per bottle for this service. Jupiter NEXT has no corkage fee.

Hosted vs. No-Host Bar

A hosted bar means you (the couple) cover the tab for your guests. A no-host bar means guests pay for their own drinks. Most weddings use a hosted bar — it's part of what makes guests feel taken care of.

Pipe & Drape

A system of metal poles and fabric panels used to divide or transform a space. At Castaway, pipe and drape is used to conceal the fully pre-set reception area until the dramatic reveal after cocktail hour.

Room Flip

When a single space is used for two different purposes — like a ceremony and a reception — and has to be rearranged in between. This takes time and is usually done while guests are at cocktail hour. Avoiding a room flip was one of your top priorities.

Rain Backup

The indoor alternative plan if weather prevents an outdoor ceremony. Every outdoor ceremony needs one. The best rain backups require no room flip and are invisible to guests — they just move inside seamlessly.

COI / Event Insurance

Certificate of Insurance — proof that you carry general liability coverage for your event. Most venues require $1,000,000 in coverage. A two-day policy costs around $127 and takes about 10 minutes to purchase online.

Day-of Coordinator

A planner who manages logistics on the wedding day itself — vendor arrivals, timeline, cues, problem-solving. Some venues require one. That's me: as your planner, day-of coordination is included in what we do together.

Rental Window

The total time you have access to the venue, including setup and breakdown. Not all of that time is "event time" — setup and cleanup come out of the window too. Always confirm what's included before signing.

Tablescape

The full visual design of your tables — linens, place settings, glassware, flatware, and tabletop florals. Higher Love sources and styles your tablescape collection; delivery is handled by our rental partner.

Gratuity

Tips for your vendors and venue staff. Unlike a service charge (which goes to the company), gratuity goes directly to the people who worked your event. It's customary and appreciated — we'll plan for it in your budget.
