Getting Married in St. Johns, Portland — A Local’s Guide

By Kylie Founder of Higher Love Event Co.

St. Johns is one of Portland’s best-kept secrets for weddings — and as someone who lives and works here, I want to help you discover why.

It’s gritty and charming, unpretentious and beautiful, with Cathedral Park and the St. Johns Bridge as its crown jewels. The kind of place that attracts couples who want something real. If that sounds like you, keep reading.

Why St. Johns?

The neighborhood has a small-town feel that’s genuinely rare in a city this size. Independent coffee shops, local restaurants, a tight-knit creative community. St. Johns has its own identity — and couples who choose it tend to feel that immediately.

Ceremony & Photo Locations

Ceremony & Photo Location

Cathedral Park

Few places stop you in your tracks the way Cathedral Park does. The Gothic arches of the St. Johns Bridge, the Willamette River, the deep green of the surrounding trees — it’s one of those backdrops that elevates everything around it. Your photographer will thank you.

A note on access: The Cathedral Park Staircase and Deck are currently unavailable for permit reservations due to St. Johns Bridge repair work. However, the Cathedral Park Amphitheater is available for reservations. If you’re having a simple ceremony with no more than the couple, officiant, and two witnesses, you may use the Staircase and Deck on a first-come, first-served basis as bridge repair work allows. For permit reservations, visit portland.gov/parks/wedding.

Photo Location

St. Johns Bridge

The St. Johns Bridge is one of those landmarks that earns its reputation every single time. A Gothic suspension bridge painted seafoam green, flanked by forest on both sides and framing the Willamette River below — it’s been one of Portland’s most photographed spots for nearly a century, and it’s easy to see why. If you want photos that are truly unlike anything else, make time for the bridge.
