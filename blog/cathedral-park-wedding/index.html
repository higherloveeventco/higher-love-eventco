Here's everything you need to know to get married here. --- ## The Venue Itself Cathedral Park sits in the St. Johns neighborhood of North Portland, directly under the eastern end of the St. Johns Bridge. The bridge was designed by David B. Steinman and first opened in 1931. At 400 feet tall, it remains Portland's tallest bridge and the only suspension bridge in the Willamette Valley. The gothic arched towers give the park its name. Stand under them and you'll immediately understand why. The park covers 21 acres along the riverfront and includes: - The iconic stairs and deck under the bridge arches (the ceremony spot couples dream about) - A 500-person capacity amphitheater with a performance stage - Riverfront access with a boat dock and beach area - Paved accessible paths and accessible restrooms - Picnic areas, an off-leash dog area, and a nature patch garden completed in 2024 The best light for photos is late afternoon or early evening before sunset — the park faces west, so golden hour hits beautifully. The bridge also provides partial rain cover, which is one of its most underrated features. The bridge provides partial rain cover, which makes Cathedral Park a more weather-resilient option than most outdoor ceremony spots in Portland. --- ## What's Happening Right Now: The Bridge Repair Project Here's the part most couples don't know about until they've already fallen for this spot. The Cathedral Park Staircase and Deck — the iconic ceremony spot under the arches — has been unavailable for permitted weddings since October 2022. ODOT has been doing concrete repair work on the pier columns under the bridge, and the construction has kept the staircase off-limits for events. The amphitheater is still fully bookable, and small elopements (couple, officiant, and two witnesses only) are still allowed on a first-come, first-served basis. The pier work is anticipated to be complete at the end of 2026, which means the staircase and deck are expected to reopen for permitted weddings in the 2027 booking cycle. This is significant. There are four-plus years of pent-up demand for this spot. Couples who dreamed of getting married here and have been waiting are going to come flooding back the moment permits are available. If you're planning a 2027 wedding, now is the time to start making moves. --- ## The 2027 Permit Situation: What to Expect Portland Parks & Recreation (PP&R) opens wedding permit reservations in early January each year for that year's dates. For popular venues like Cathedral Park, it is a literal line-up event. Couples (and their planners) show up outside the Portland Building at 1120 SW 5th Ave before 8 AM on the first day of reservations to secure their date. Phone and online requests open the following day, but by then many Saturdays are already gone. Key logistics: - Permits must be requested at least 45 days in advance to avoid rush fees - Pre-closure pricing for the staircase and deck was $621.50 for up to 100 guests - A Z50 key and key deposit are required for electricity access, which is located about 150 feet from the ceremony space - Portable restrooms are recommended, as the park's permanent restrooms are lower in the park, not close to the stairs - The staircase and deck are not ADA accessible When January 2027 rolls around, I'll be at the Portland Building with my couples on permit day.
